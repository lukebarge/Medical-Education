Breathing

The rhythmic expansion and contraction of the lungs that exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide between blood and the atmosphere.

breathing.js — live simulation

Overview

Normal quiet breathing — eupnea — involves the diaphragm descending and the thoracic cage expanding to create a slightly negative intrathoracic pressure. Air then flows in passively along the pressure gradient. Expiration at rest is largely passive, driven by the elastic recoil of lung tissue.

At rest, an adult takes 12–20 breaths per minute with a tidal volume of approximately 500 mL. This gives a minute ventilation of roughly 6–10 L/min.

⚠️
Airway resistance and Poiseuille's Law Resistance to airflow varies with the fourth power of radius. Halving airway diameter increases resistance 16-fold. Drag the Airway Resistance slider to simulate bronchospasm as seen in asthma or COPD.

Mechanics of Breathing

⬇️
Inspiration
Diaphragm and external intercostals contract, increasing thoracic volume and lowering alveolar pressure below atmospheric.
⬆️
Expiration
At rest, passive. Recoil of stretched lung tissue restores thoracic volume. Active during exercise or airway obstruction.
💨
Compliance
The ease with which lungs expand. Reduced in fibrosis; increased in emphysema where alveolar walls are destroyed.

Parameters

Clinical Relevance

Asthma increases airway resistance through bronchospasm, inflammation, and mucus. Expiration becomes effortful and prolonged. COPD combines airway obstruction with loss of elastic recoil, causing air trapping and hyperinflation.

Pulmonary fibrosis reduces lung compliance, so greater muscular effort is required to achieve the same tidal volume — reflected as rapid, shallow breathing. Try setting a high airway resistance and low tidal volume to simulate an obstructive breathing pattern.
