Pharmacokinetics

The mathematical description of how a drug moves through the body over time — absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (ADME).

pharmacokinetics.js — live simulation

Overview

The concentration-time (C–t) curve plotted above is generated using the one-compartment Bateman equation, which models oral absorption followed by first-order elimination:

C(t) = Cmax · (e−ket − e−kat)

The orange dashed lines mark Cmax (peak concentration) and tmax (time to peak). The purple line marks one elimination half-life after tmax.

ℹ️
The simulation runs at accelerated speed. Each real second represents several hours of drug exposure. Use the Restart button after changing parameters to see the updated curve.

Key Concepts

📈
Cmax
Peak plasma concentration. Must exceed the minimum effective concentration (MEC) for therapeutic effect.
⏱️
t½ (Half-Life)
Time for plasma concentration to halve. Determines dosing interval. Steady state is reached after ~5 half-lives.
📦
Vd
Volume of distribution. Apparent volume in which drug is distributed. High Vd means extensive tissue binding.
🔢
AUC
Area under the curve — total drug exposure. Proportional to dose/clearance. Used to compare formulations and bioavailability.

Parameters

ParameterExampleEffect on curve
Cmax 100 mg/L Shifts curve vertically. Higher Cmax → increased AUC and risk of toxicity.
Half-life (t½) 4 h Steeper or shallower elimination slope. Longer t½ → drug persists longer → accumulation with repeat dosing.
tmax 1 h Time of peak. Delayed by food, slow-release formulations. Reduced for sublingual/IV routes.
Vd 30 L Affects relationship between plasma concentration and total body drug. Warfarin Vd ~8 L; chloroquine ~200–800 L.

Clinical Relevance

Therapeutic drug monitoring (e.g., digoxin, gentamicin, lithium) uses trough and peak plasma concentrations to ensure levels remain within the therapeutic window — above the MEC and below the minimum toxic concentration (MTC).

Renal impairment reduces elimination (increases effective half-life) — drag the half-life slider upward to simulate drug accumulation in a patient with reduced creatinine clearance. This is particularly important for renally excreted drugs with narrow therapeutic indices, such as aminoglycosides.
